Fire incidents in Boston

Written by Shuang Jing

Fire incidents can have devastating consequences, causing property damage, injuries, and loss of life. As a result, cities around the world invest significant resources in fire prevention and response. In the city of Boston, the fire department responds to an average of 40,000 incidents per year, ranging from small fires to major emergencies. In this project, I will analyze data from the Boston Fire Incident Reporting System for the period from April 2, 2022, to April 1, 2023. By exploring the patterns and trends in fire incidents, I wish to provide insights that can inform fire prevention strategies and improve emergency response in the city.

Data analysis

We conducted an exploratory analysis of the fire incidents in Boston from April 2, 2022 to April 1, 2023. The dataset contained information on the date, time, location, and type of each fire incident.

The fire incident data used in this analysis was obtained from the Boston Fire Department. The data includes information on the date, time, location, and cause of each fire incident that occurred during the selected time period.

1. Number of fires in each neighbor in Boston

The data collected between April 2, 2022, to April 1, 2023 reveals that the neighborhoods of Boston, Charlestown, and Roxbury were the areas most affected by fires. The incidence of fires in these areas was significantly higher than other neighborhoods in the Boston region.

The most frequent time and month for fire incidents

The fourth visualization is a bar chart that displays the number of fire alarm calls per month and per hour. It shows that displays the number of fire alarms reported in each month and each hour of the day over the past year. The chart reveals that the frequency of fire alarms in February was the highest of all months, likely due to the large fire at the Brookline restaurant as discussed earlier. Additionally, the data shows that the months of July and August had the highest number of fire alarms reported. Interestingly, the chart also displays a higher frequency of fire alarms being reported during midday and afternoon hours compared to morning and evening hours.This pattern could be attributed to more activities taking place during the day, such as cooking, heating, and using electrical appliances.
